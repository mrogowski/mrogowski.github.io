High Performance Computing

Jun 30, 2021

I first became interested in HPC in 2011 during my M.S. coursework. After a brief Human-Computer Interaction episode, I returned to HPC in 2014 and worked in this field ever since. Between 2014 and 2019, I worked on Saudi Aramco’s reservoir simulator - GigaPOWERS and had the chance to lead the performance optimization records. During that time, I had the chance to run on several TOP500-ranked supercomputers and achieve a few milestones: evaluate the scalability up to 196'609 cores back in 2017, and was involved in record-setting 1-billion-cell simulations.

As part of those projects I gained extensive experience with domain partitioning, matrix-vector multiplication optimization, OpenMP, fast compression algorithms, MPI tuning and asynchronous I/O.

From 2020, I started to work on an h/p/r-adaptive framework developed at KAUST’s AANSLab used to simulate turbulent compressible internal and external flows on unstructured grids for industrial applications. I focus on the solver’s performance, communication, and scalability.
